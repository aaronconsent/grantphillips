About

The long version.

Grant Dewell Phillips is a double bassist and Master of Music candidate at the Michigan State University College of Music. He has performed continuously since middle school, building a reputation for reliability, musicianship, and a tone that anchors any ensemble he joins.

Biography

From a first instrument to a graduate degree.

Grant Phillips' path to the double bass began with a move. In 2008, his family relocated to Houston, Texas, where he enrolled in a school that asked every student to commit to an artistic discipline. He chose the double bass — a decision that would change the course of his life.

Under the guidance of his first teacher, Charles Tittsworth, that early choice grew into a serious pursuit. Through elementary and middle school, Grant rose to first chair again and again, building the foundation that would carry him into one of the country's most competitive arts high schools. At Houston's renowned Kinder High School for the Performing and Visual Arts (HSPVA), he continued his studies with Eric Larson, a double bassist of the Houston Symphony — training with a working professional that helped propel him to the highest level of student music in Texas. He was named to the Texas All-State Symphonic Band in 2018 and the Texas All-State Symphony Orchestra in 2019, the top tier of a grueling, multi-round audition process, performing both years at the Texas Music Educators Association convention in San Antonio.

After graduating from HSPVA, Grant took an ambitious next step that reflected both sides of his mind. At the University of Michigan, he pursued a dual major in astrophysics and orchestral music, completing both degrees over five years while studying double bass in the studio of Professor Nicholas Walker — a Fulbright Scholar and past president of the International Society of Bassists. In November 2023, he gave a solo recital at U-M's School of Music, Theatre & Dance.

Today, Grant continues his journey at Michigan State University, performing with orchestras throughout the Ann Arbor and Lansing area — still following the instrument he chose as a kid newly arrived in Houston, and still letting it carry him somewhere new.
