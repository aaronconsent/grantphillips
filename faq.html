FAQ

Common questions.

Quick answers about pricing, coverage, instrumentation, and what to expect when you book Grant for a performance.

What does Grant charge for a wedding?
Flat-rate quotes are sent within one business day of inquiry. Pricing depends on travel distance, total time on site, and whether amplification is required. Most central-Michigan ceremony-and-cocktail-hour packages fall in a predictable range — contact for a current quote.
What's the coverage area?
Routine bookings throughout a roughly 100-mile radius of East Lansing, MI — including Lansing, Grand Rapids, Kalamazoo, Battle Creek, Jackson, Ann Arbor, the Detroit metro, and Flint. Dates further afield are possible with enough lead time.
Can Grant play in a duo, trio, or quartet?
Yes. Solo double bass, bass-and-cello duo, jazz trio (piano/bass/drums or guitar/bass/drums), and string quartet with added bass are all available. Grant maintains relationships with collaborators across Michigan and can build the right ensemble for the event.
What musical styles does he cover?
Classical (orchestral standard repertoire, chamber, baroque continuo), sacred music (hymns, liturgical, Holy Week, Christmas), jazz (standards, bebop, swing, bossa, brushes-and-bass duos), and arranged accompaniment for pop, folk, and singer-songwriter work.
Does he provide his own amplification?
Yes. For outdoor ceremonies, larger reception spaces, and most jazz rooms, Grant brings a high-quality combo amp suited to upright bass. For acoustic chamber and orchestral work, no amplification is needed.
How far in advance should I book?
Weddings book best 3-12 months out. Church services typically 2-6 weeks. Jazz and restaurant dates often 1-4 weeks. Orchestral subbing can be confirmed on as little as 48 hours' notice.
Is a deposit required?
No deposit is required to check a date. A non-refundable retainer is requested to confirm the booking; remainder due on the day of the event.
What does Grant wear to a performance?
Standard attire is black tie or all-black formal. For specific dress codes (white tie, cocktail, themed events), let him know during booking.
