What makes a bassist easy to work with at a church.

  • Reads anything you put in front of him — score, hymnal, chord chart, lead sheet.
  • Knows when to drop out. A trained bassist supports a cappella moments by stepping back, not muscling through.
  • Tunes with the organ in advance — arrives 60 minutes before downbeat.
  • Black-attire ready for processional or balcony placement.
  • Quiet logistics. One trip in, no spectacle, gone by the time fellowship starts.
