A double bass changes how a room feels.

Live, acoustic, warm — and unlike a quartet or DJ, the bass shapes the air without competing with conversation. For Michigan couples planning a ceremony and reception, Grant Phillips offers solo bass and ensemble options that travel beautifully from chapel to cocktail hour.

Three places the bass fits a wedding day.

Ceremony

Prelude · Processional · Recessional

Pachelbel Canon, Bach Air, Wagner Bridal Chorus, contemporary arrangements — solo bass, or paired with cello, harp, or violin. Outdoor ceremonies fully supported with amplification.

Cocktail Hour

Background that elevates

Jazz standards, bossa nova, light classical, or arranged pop. Bass-led trio or duo holds the room at the perfect volume — present, not pushy.

Reception

Dinner & toasts

Quiet enough for speeches, lively enough for the room. Curated set lists per couple, with first-dance and special-request arrangements available.

Common formats Michigan couples request.

  • Solo double bass — intimate ceremonies, courthouse weddings, micro-weddings.
  • Bass & cello duo — the sweet spot for processionals and small chapels.
  • Bass & guitar duo — vineyard, barn, and outdoor ceremonies; great for jazz cocktail hours.
  • Jazz trio (piano/bass/drums) — cocktail hour and dinner reception.
  • String quartet + bass — full-bodied processional sound for cathedral and larger venues.

What couples ask.

Can you play our specific song?
Yes — most popular and classical wedding pieces are in the standard book, and custom arrangements are available with two weeks of lead time.
Do you travel to our venue?
Anywhere within roughly 100 miles of East Lansing is routine — Grand Rapids, Ann Arbor, Detroit metro, Kalamazoo. Further dates possible; ask.
Will you coordinate with our planner / officiant?
Always. A short call or email exchange the week of the event covers timing, processional cues, and any special moments.
